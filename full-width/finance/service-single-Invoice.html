How can Adsum help with Invoice Financing?

We've tried to make the process really easy for our customers. Of course that doesn't mean that we don't perform checks but we are very focused on providing you with a simple and speedy way to convert your invoice receivables into cash. This is how it works from start to finish.

2. Get a Quote

Within 24 hours Alex at Adsum will respond to you with our proposal.
Example: 1 Invoice for $10,000, payable within 30 days – Alex @ Adsum via WhatsApp:
“Hey! Thanks for your invoice. We can advance 70% upfront at 1.0% per month. Would you like to proceed?” This means that you will receive $7,000 from Adsum and then receive the remaining $2,900 ($10,000-$7,000-$100) when your customer pays in 30 days time.

Once you’ve confirmed that you would like to proceed by e-signing your proposal, Adsum will request the authorised signatory list for your company along with contact details for your customer so we can contact them directly to confirm invoice validity and invite them to comfirm that they will pay the invoice directly to Adsum. When we receive payment from your customers we will pay your balance across to the account details provided on the original invoice. Frequently Asked Questions
What will my costumers think?

Working capital finance and invoice discounting is one of the oldest and most common forms of trade financing. The chances are that your customers are already doing it themselves. In days gone by, companies relied on overdrafts and fixed-term facilities to meet their financing needs but increasingly businesses like yours don’t want to pay fees on long-term facilities that they don’t need. Just think of the serviced-office market. Nobody knows where their business will be in 20 years so why would sign a 20 year office lease but that’s exactly what people used to do before the arrival of WeWork where you pay for the office space you need, when and where you need it. This is partly what we mean by the ‘democratisation of capital’. Your customers know that your ability access capital makes you a stronger counter-party and that by so doing you are ensuring that they receive the best terms and service from you. Invoice discounting is the most common way to finance trade and to support growth. Your customers know this too. We dont hassle your clients for payment – we leave this up to you and the business relationship remains entirely in your hands unless you ask us to help but that’s up to you to decide. The only contact we have with your customers is when we verify invoices and we have developed a quick and easy way for them to do this.

How will this impact my tax position?

Invoice discounting is tax neutral for our customers. We purchase your invoices for a small discount to their ‘face value’ meaning that ‘interest’ is not charged. Consequently our service is sharia compliant. It also means that there are no local sales tax consequences for our customers. In effect we purchase the right to receive payment from your customer in return for a small discount to the amount that you will ultimately receive. You account for any relevant local sales taxes to your local tax office when payment is received as usual and in all other respects you maintain an ordinary working relationship with your customer. It couldn’t be simpler.

Which invoices are financeable?

We can finance all of your invoices regardless of where you are based provided your customers are based in the ‘West’ (US, Europe, Australia, Singapore, etc) and provided the average size of the invoices that you want to finance is greater than $10,000. Our speciality is international trade financing – in other words financing exporters of goods and services. Many of our customers operate both locally and internationally, exporting to customers in the ‘West’ and locally. While we get to know each another we can only finance your export invoices but once we get to know you we can finance your domestic invoices too. If you’ve got any queries about eligibility, just drop us a WhatsApp and we can explain further.

What benefits factoring provides?

Invoice discounting (also known as factoring) is one of the oldest forms of trade financing. Because of it’s simplicity it is an easier and quicker way for a business to access funding. Many businesses use factoring as a means to fund growth. A business can only grow at the speed that it can generate additional working capital and yet many growth businesses end up absorbing capital because of the delay between shipping goods (or providing services) and receiving payment from your customers. That’s why people say that factoring ‘oils the wheels of trade’.
