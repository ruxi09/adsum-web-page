How Do I Know If My Property Qualifies For VAT?

  • By J T
  • Posted On October 16th, 2020

One question frequently asked by developers and commercial finance brokers is “How do I know if my property qualifies for VAT?” And, while it’s a simple question, the answer is rather complicated. Fortunately, Justina Tartilaite, director of AdSum is on hand to provide some helpful guidance on how to discover a property’s vat status so you know when you must register your property and how. How does someone know if their property qualifies for VAT or not?

When considering the question, we need to take a look at the basic requirement for VAT status and whether your property is subject to Value Added Tax (VAT) on the sale price or the rent. This is especially important as it could have a significant effect on the overall purchase price, or the amount of rent paid on a lease of the property. Any time that you sell or rent an existing residential property, you don’t charge VAT but cannot recover the VAT you incur on your purchases relating to it.

Is the purchase or a lease of commercial property is exempt from VAT?

For most cases, the purchase or a lease of commercial property is exempt from VAT. With that being said, there are some exceptions to this rule. These include:
· A sale of commercial property where the seller/landlord has opted to charge VAT.
· A sale of a freehold interest in an uncompleted commercial building.
· A sale of commercial property built less than three years before the sale.

Can you register for VAT if you let domestic property only?

Typically, if your only business activity is letting domestic property, you are not allowed to register for VAT.

Does this rule apply to the construction of a new domestic property?

You will have created a zero-rated supply if you construct a new domestic property and then sell the Freehold. That means you are allowed to recover VAT on construction costs. However, if you let this newly constructed property, you will not be able to recover the VAT you were charged on your costs because you will have created an exempt supply.

What if the seller/landlord opted to charge VAT?

If a seller or landlord has decided to charge VAT to recover VAT charged on any costs related to the property, that decision binds all buyers within the same VAT group, and it is typically irrevocable. There are some exceptions to that rule, of course, such as when a building is demolished, or within six months of deciding to opt to charge VAT. There are certain conditions that must be met before having the original decision to charge VAT overturned.
